What is Agentic AI - From automation to autonomous

Agentic AI uses autonomous AI agents (that can Think, Plan, Reason and Act) to redefine how to interact with systems and apps. These agents can perform complex tasks, make decisions, and adapt to changing environments, enabling businesses to automate workflows, enhance customer experiences, and drive innovation at scale. They are different from previous generation Chatbots and generative AI assistant that primarily focus on text generation and conversation. Below are key characteristics of Agentic AI systems:

Agentic AI Maturity Model for Enterprises

1

The Reactive Chatbot (Knowledge Retrieval)

State: The enterprise uses generic LLMs (ChatGPT/Claude) for basic summarization and drafting.

Limitation: "The Island of Knowledge." It knows things but can't do anything. Data is siloed and requires manual copying/pasting.

VisionTomoro Focus: Context Ingestion & Security Guardrails.

2

The Grounded Assistant (RAG Enabled)

State: AI is connected to internal company documents (Retrieval-Augmented Generation). It answers questions based on your data.

Limitation: It is still reactive. A human must ask a question to get a result. It cannot execute transactions.

VisionTomoro Focus: Data Fabric Engineering & Vector Database Optimization.

3

The Task-Specific Agent (System of Action)

State: The AI is given "Tools" (APIs, SQL access). It can perform specific tasks, like "Update a CRM record" or "Generate a Jira ticket."

Limitation: Restricted to single-turn tasks. It lacks the "Reasoning" to handle complex, multi-step projects.

VisionTomoro Focus: Tool-Use Architecture & Intent Engineering.

4

The Multi-Agent System (Orchestrated Workflows)

State: Multiple specialized agents (e.g., a "Researcher Agent," a "Coder Agent," and a "QA Agent") work together to solve a high-level goal.

Limitation: Requires heavy human supervision and "AgentOps" to prevent loops or errors.

VisionTomoro Focus: Multi-Agent Orchestration (LangGraph/CrewAI) & Agentic DevOps.

5

The Agentic Enterprise (Fully Autonomous)

State: Self-learning agents manage end-to-end business processes (e.g., autonomous claims processing or self-healing cloud infra). Humans move to a "Governor" role.

Advantage: Zero-latency execution. The enterprise operates at the speed of light, not the speed of meetings.

VisionTomoro Focus: AgentOps Lifecycle Management & Neo-Operating Model Design.

Multi agent orchestration patterns

Multi-agent orchestration patterns define the architecture and communication strategies for coordinating multiple AI agents working together to solve complex problems. These patterns enable enterprises to design scalable, maintainable, and efficient agentic systems.

Key orchestration patterns include sequential task execution, parallel processing, hierarchical control structures, and peer-to-peer collaboration. Each pattern is optimized for different use cases and complexity levels.

VisionTomoro specializes in implementing these patterns using industry-leading frameworks and best practices, ensuring your multi-agent systems operate with maximum efficiency and reliability.
